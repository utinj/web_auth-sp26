About The Project

Trash is everywhere. It permeates our lives in a world of objects, where people decide the fates of the goods around us. On this site, I want to explore the world of trash, and how trash influences our world. We like to think that trash is something that can be ignored and thrown 'away', but unfortunately, that is far from the truth. Landfills dot the world, harming people, animals, and the planet as a whole. Throughout our lives, it seems like we keep piling on more and more garbage in a never-ending cycle.

As such, I hope to dive into my own trash and habits regarding garbage within weekly posts here on Trash Told Timely. Trash as a topic is much more nuanced than what it appears to be on the surface. As the proverb "One man's trash is another man's treasure" goes, the line between what things can be called trash and things that can be called treasure is often blurry and subjective. The sheer number of things in people's lives that ring true to this proverb would be too many to count. However, I can count my own trash, and perhaps that can unviel some of the mystery surrounding our trash habits.

With that being said, trash does not have to be an inevitability. Some trash can truly be consdered treasure; perhaps some objects have more stories to tell. As Vik Muniz puts it, garbage can and should be its own art. By documenting and exploring the stories of such garbage, I want to be able to appreciate the things we throw away just a bit more and give them a little more life. In other ways, perhaps trash did not have to become the feared, inescapable product of our lives. In fact, there may be more to the concept of trash itself, and that there is a time where trash didn't need to exist.

While I won't be able to eliminate the garbage problems of the world, I hope I can provide some insights in the trash of our daily lives.

Tinjitrash

I'm Tinjitrash, a dedicated environmental awareness advocator on the mission to explore our livelihoods of trash. As someone who is fascinated with the world of product and items around us, and the impacts of our things on the world, I want to reach out to as many people as possible about the way we think about stuff.

As someone who cares about my own trash, I tend to be a hoarder. I have many things around my home that often see little use and are likely targets to be trashed, but I have a hard time following through into throwing stuff away. Through documentation and data collection, I want to be more diligent about my trash, and share my findings about their stories!
